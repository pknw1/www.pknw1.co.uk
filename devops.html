& Digital Transformation

DevOps is the correlation of people, process, and technology to enable continuous delivery of value to end users.
Success relies on 4 key factors; I've broken down each into focus areas which I have seen make a difference during my time implementing a move towards a DevOps methodology

                     

Focus Areas for Delivery Success

  • People
  • Process
  • Technology
  • Feedback

Inventory

 Understand and list all the steps and inputs/involvement for processes with SME's, business and existing processes / procedures

Ownership

 Ensure business, technical and approval ownership for your work so that any blockers canbe overcome with their input

Integrations

Learn the process inputs, data, outputs, volumes, timings etc and be mindful of what is normal or abnormal so we can automate with confidence and intervene if non-standard deviation is observed

Priority

Understand where the process is in the system lifecycle and it's importance to day-to-day operation and the benefit it will provide to automate and transform

Capability

When choosing technology or imoplementing a solution, consider how well equiped the business is to support it without you; consider whether other options or upskill could benefit more

Support

How well supported is a technology or solution - do we need and can we cost in support for opensource products - can we use just community support?

Impact   

  How usable is a technology choice across the business; can we use its function and capability elsewhere, improving it's value overall?

Complexity

 How complex is a solution? Have we seen other projects on Github etc showing success - and what do the user comments tell us about how others have found the product?

Monitor Everything

Consider minimum, maximum, normal and trend data for process inputs, processing and outputs so that we can watch and tune triggers for alerting at every stage

Feedback

 Question process stages for whether they are legacy or still required? Do elements still need approval from SecOps or others if we already use approved build steps

Take Ownership

 Always assume no-one else has seen what you are seeing; bring others in and ensure that business, tech owners as well as incident managers etc are aware and communicating

Trending

Know your system and how it performs; Monitor and review changes in performance and alerts raised etc and map them to well versioned releases to identify sources for performance and capability
