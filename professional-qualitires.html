Culture

What Culture and Work Practices Will I bring?

 

1

First Principles Thinking

I've used an extensive range of technology in my career and not only maintain a wide range of core skills, but have become adept at quickly adapting or acquiring new skills as challenges approach. Technologies appear and evolve at such a pace that understanding a challenge and how to apply yourself is far more valuable than reciting set-piece processes

2

Experience across disciplines

My career has taken me across industry sectors, engagement models and a huge array of architectures and delivery models - involved at every stage I find that my ability to break processes, systems, workflows or queries into well defined elements that can be clearly defined and understood is one of the most useful skills to bring to an agile environment

3

Innovative Thinking 

I am always exploring what the technology communities around us have to offer - exploring github solutions or issues, trying new features, mixing technologies - and this has nurtured my thinking to reflect a global collection of ideas in new and interesting ways 
My personal projects have seen me explore tools or tech that would not normally present themselves in the work place - so when new challenges arrive at work, I have a wider set of experiences or thought processes to call on than many engineers

4

Passion for Technology and People

While my passion for using the right tools for the best outcome has been a consistent theme throughout my career, as I've progressed I have considered the people aspect of systems as much as the technology. The users and their experiences; Engineers skills, growth and also the business rationale that can drive solutions in ways that a pure technology focused individual could be unaware of. I can explain and motivate by delivering thought processes or decisions in ways that ensure positive engagement

5

Open, Honest & Targeted Communication

The most common reason for failure or deficiency in a system often comes due to either mis-communication, over-commitment, or a fear of asking for clarification. I am always thinking how to ensure any requirement or issue is refined into terms that is unambiguous, clear and appropriate for its intended audience
